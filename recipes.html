Blini (Russian Crepes)

These large, thin pancakes are perfect for pairing with sweet or savory toppings.

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, sugar, and salt.
  2. Gradually add the flour, mixing until smooth and lump-free.
  3. Stir in the melted butter or oil.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and lightly grease with butter.
  5. Pour a small amount of batter into the pan, swirling to cover the surface.
  6. Cook for 1-2 minutes until edges lift, then flip and cook for another minute.
  7. Serve with jam, sour cream, or condensed milk. Enjoy!

Olivier (Russian Salad)

A classic Russian salad with potatoes, carrots, ham, and peas, perfect for any occasion.

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the potatoes, carrots, ham (or chicken), eggs, peas, and pickles.
  2. Add the mayonnaise, and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Mix gently until well combined.
  4. Serve chilled, and enjoy this classic Russian salad!

Borsch (Beet Soup)

A hearty beet soup with a mix of vegetables, perfect for a comforting meal.

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the broth to a boil and add the potato and bay leaf. Cook for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the beets, carrot, cabbage, onion, and garlic. Simmer until vegetables are tender.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, and season with salt and pepper. Simmer for another 10 minutes.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with dill or parsley and a dollop of sour cream.

Tajik Cuisine

Shakarob (Tajik Salad)

A refreshing mix of tomatoes, cucumber, and naan bread, perfect for a hot day.

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, onion, and bread pieces.
  2. Add the fresh herbs and salt to taste, then gently mix everything together.
  3. Optional: Add a spoonful of yogurt or sour cream for a creamy texture.
  4. Serve immediately as a light meal or side dish.

Mantu (Tajik Dumplings)

Hearty dumplings filled with ground meat, served with yogurt.

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and water until a smooth dough forms. Let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling: In another bowl, mix the ground meat, onion, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Assemble Mantu: Roll the dough thin and cut it into 3-inch squares. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each square, then fold and seal the edges to form a dumpling.
  4. Steam: Place the mantu in a steamer, leaving space between each. Steam for 30-40 minutes until cooked through.
  5. Serve: Top with yogurt or sour cream and garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

Sambusa (Tajik Pastry)

A delicious stuffed pastry filled with ground meat, perfect as a snack or appetizer.

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a bowl, combine flour, salt, oil, and water, kneading until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling: In another bowl, mix the ground meat, onion, salt, pepper, cumin, and herbs.
  3. Assemble Sambusa: Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one into a thin circle. Place a spoonful of filling in the center, then fold the dough into a triangle shape, sealing the edges.
  4. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the sambusas on a baking sheet, brush with beaten egg, and sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy these flavorful pastries!

Pamiri Cuisine

Osh (Pamiri Plov)

A flavorful dish of rice and meat with carrots, garlic, and spices, perfect for a family meal.

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Meat: In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the meat and cook until browned.
  2. Add Vegetables: Add the onion, garlic, and carrot to the pot. Cook until the onion is translucent and the carrot is softened.
  3. Season and Add Rice: Stir in the tomato, cumin, salt, and pepper. Add the rice and mix well with the meat and vegetables.
  4. Simmer: Add enough water to cover the rice and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and let it simmer until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed (about 20-25 minutes).
  5. Serve: Fluff the rice with a fork, garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley, and serve warm.

Bichak (Pamiri Pastry)

A versatile Pamiri pastry that can be filled with sweet or savory ingredients, perfect for any time of the day.

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a bowl, combine flour, salt, oil, and water, kneading until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Make the Filling: For savory, mix mashed potatoes with cheese, salt, and pepper. For sweet, mix the pumpkin or sweet potato with sugar and cinnamon.
  3. Assemble Bichak: Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one into a thin circle. Place a spoonful of filling in the center, fold into a half-moon shape, and seal the edges.
  4. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the bichak on a baking sheet, brush with beaten egg, and sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired. Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy these tasty Pamiri pastries!

Shurbo (Pamiri Soup)

A hearty soup made with lamb or beef, vegetables, and spices, perfect for a cozy meal.

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Meat: In a large pot, brown the meat over medium heat until lightly seared.
  2. Add Vegetables: Add the onion, garlic, and tomato to the pot. Cook until the onion is translucent.
  3. Simmer: Add the carrots, potato, bell pepper, salt, pepper, and cumin. Pour in the water or broth and bring to a boil.
  4. Cook Until Tender: Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 1-1.5 hours until the meat is tender and flavors blend.
  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh dill or cilantro and enjoy this warm, hearty soup!
